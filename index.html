About Us

KFC History

KFC’s origins trace back to 1930, when Harland Sanders opened a small service station restaurant in Corbin, Kentucky, during the Great Depression. Initially, Sanders served traditional American food, but his fried chicken became particularly popular. Over the next decade, he refined his cooking technique, developing a secret recipe of 11 herbs and spices in 1940 that made his chicken stand out. He used a pressure cooker to reduce cooking time without sacrificing flavor or texture, a technique that revolutionized fast food.In 1952, Sanders franchised his recipe for the first time to Pete Harman in Salt Lake City, Utah, marking the birth of Kentucky Fried Chicken as a brand. The restaurant’s sales tripled after adding KFC to the menu. Sanders, in his signature white suit and black string tie, became a symbol of the brand, and he began traveling the country, pitching his franchise model to restaurant owners. By the mid-1960s, KFC had expanded internationally, opening locations in Canada, the U.K., and beyond. In 1964, at age 73, Sanders sold the company for $2 million to a group of investors led by John Y. Brown and Jack Massey, though he remained the face of KFC as its brand ambassador.
